POBs reveals how different LLMs respond to subjective, cultural, and ethical questions.

Abstract

We present the Preference, Opinion, and Belief survey (POBs), a benchmark for evaluating LLMs' subjectivity across societal, ethical, and personal domains. We examine how increasing test-time compute—through reasoning and self-reflection—impacts reliability, neutrality, and consistency. Our findings show that while these mechanisms help in other tasks, they offer limited improvements here. Surprisingly, newer models tend to be less consistent and more biased, often aligning with progressive-collectivist views. POBs enables deeper auditing of LLM behavior, helping users and developers better understand and manage ideological tendencies in deployed models.
